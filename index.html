PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD

The system is simple: work within a rep range, reach the top, add weight. Repeat forever. This is called double progression and it is the most research-backed method for beginners. Compound exercises use 8-12 reps. Isolation exercises (curls, lateral raises) use higher ranges as noted on the session page.

1
Pick your starting weight
Choose a weight where you can do 8 clean reps but a 9th would be a real struggle. If you can breeze past 12, it is too light. If you cannot reach 8 with good form, it is too heavy.
2
Warm-up set
10 reps at roughly 50% of your working weight. Easy. This primes the muscle and the movement pattern without tiring you out. Rest 60 seconds, then begin.
3
3 working sets of 8-12 reps
Use the same weight for all 3 sets. Stop each set 1-2 reps before you would genuinely fail — meaning you could have done one more rep, but you chose not to. This is the correct intensity. Going all the way to failure every set adds unnecessary fatigue without meaningfully more growth. Rest 90 seconds to 2 minutes between sets.
4
Log your reps
Note what you got on each set in the Tracker tab. Your reps will naturally drop across sets — that is normal. Getting 12, 10, 9 is fine. Getting 12, 12, 12 means the weight is too light.
5
When to add weight
When you hit 12 reps on all 3 working sets in the same session, add the smallest weight increment your gym has next time. Usually 2.5 kg on machines. Start back at 8 reps with the new weight and work back up to 12. That is one full cycle.
6
Deload every 6-8 weeks
Drop all weights by about 20% for one week and keep the same sets and reps. This lets your joints and nervous system recover from accumulated fatigue. You will almost always come back stronger the following week.

SESSION TRACKER

Log your 3 working sets each session. When you hit 12 reps on all 3, add weight next time.

TIPS

NUTRITION
Calories
Weight in lbs x 14 (sedentary) = maintenance. Eat 500 kcal below that to lose fat. Example: 190 lbs x 14 = 2,660. Target: ~2,160 kcal.
Protein
0.7 to 1g per lb of bodyweight per day. Without it, a calorie deficit burns muscle instead of fat. Target: 140 to 160g daily. Chicken, eggs, Greek yoghurt, tuna, cottage cheese, protein shakes.
Flip your plate
Make protein the main portion and carbs the side. No need to cut carbs, just reduce them relative to protein. Easiest change you can make.
Liquid calories
Juice, soda, energy drinks, and fancy coffees can quietly add 500+ kcal a day. Switch to water or black coffee. Usually the fastest win available.
Default meals
Pick 2 or 3 high protein meals you enjoy, calculate the calories once, and rotate them. Removes daily decision-making and the temptation that comes with it.
Keep junk out of the house
Willpower runs out. If it is not in the fridge it cannot be eaten at 11pm. Buy better food at the shop and the problem mostly solves itself.
LIFESTYLE
Sleep
Muscle is built during sleep, not during the workout. Poor sleep raises cortisol, reduces testosterone, and makes overeating much more likely. Protect 7 to 8 hours.
Walk daily
10,000 steps burns calories without spiking hunger the way running does. A slow 30 minute walk counts. One of the easiest additions you can make.
Stress
Chronic stress stores fat and drives overeating. Sleep, rest days, and walking all help. Worth managing as seriously as diet and training.
Never miss twice
One bad day is fine and expected. Two in a row starts becoming a habit. Whatever happened, get back on it the next day.
MINDSET
Identity over goals
A goal ends when you hit it. Thinking of yourself as someone who looks after their health leads to better long term decisions than chasing a number on a scale.
No forbidden foods
Pizza, ice cream, a night out. None of these matter if the rest of the week is solid. Cutting everything out is what causes binges, not what prevents them.
Consistency over perfection
Showing up 80% of the time for 6 months beats being perfect for 3 weeks. Set a standard you can actually hit most days and stick to it.
