PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD

If the weight is not going up over time, the muscle is not growing.

1
Find your starting weight
For each exercise, pick a weight where 8 reps takes you to near failure. Rep 9 should not be possible with good form.
2
Warm-up set
10-12 reps at roughly 50% of your working weight. Easy. Rest 2 minutes before your first working set.
3
Two working sets of 8
Both sets go to near failure. You stop at 8 because a 9th rep is not there, not because you chose to stop. Rest 2-3 minutes between sets.
4
Final set to failure
Go until you cannot complete another clean rep. Log the number. Next session, try to beat it.
5
When to increase weight
If you complete both working sets and feel a 9th rep was possible, the weight is too light. Add the smallest increment available next session and repeat.
6
Deload every 6 weeks
Drop all weights by 20% for one week, then push again. Keeps joints healthy and usually leads to a stronger return.

SESSION TRACKER

Log your sets each session. The tracker tells you when to push for 9 or add weight.

TIPS

NUTRITION
Calories
Weight in lbs x 14 (sedentary) = maintenance. Eat 20% below that to lose fat. Example: 160 lbs x 14 = 2,240. Target: 1,790 kcal.
Protein
0.7 to 1g per lb of bodyweight per day. Without it, a calorie deficit burns muscle instead of fat. Chicken, eggs, Greek yoghurt, tuna, cottage cheese, protein shakes.
Flip your plate
Make protein the main portion and carbs the side. No need to cut carbs, just reduce them relative to protein. Easiest change you can make.
Liquid calories
Juice, soda, energy drinks, and fancy coffees can quietly add 500+ kcal a day. Switch to water or black coffee. Usually the fastest win available.
Default meals
Pick 2 or 3 high protein meals you enjoy, calculate the calories once, and rotate them. Removes daily decision-making and the temptation that comes with it.
Keep junk out of the house
Willpower runs out. If it is not in the fridge it cannot be eaten at 11pm. Buy better food at the shop and the problem mostly solves itself.
LIFESTYLE
Sleep
Muscle is built during sleep, not during the workout. Poor sleep raises cortisol, reduces testosterone, and makes overeating much more likely. Protect 7 to 8 hours.
Walk daily
10,000 steps burns calories without spiking hunger the way running does. A slow 30 minute walk counts. One of the easiest additions you can make.
Stress
Chronic stress stores fat and drives overeating. Sleep, rest days, and walking all help. Worth managing as seriously as diet and training.
Never miss twice
One bad day is fine and expected. Two in a row starts becoming a habit. Whatever happened, get back on it the next day.
MINDSET
Identity over goals
A goal ends when you hit it. Thinking of yourself as someone who looks after their health leads to better long term decisions than chasing a number on a scale.
No forbidden foods
Pizza, ice cream, a night out. None of these matter if the rest of the week is solid. Cutting everything out is what causes binges, not what prevents them.
Consistency over perfection
Showing up 80% of the time for 6 months beats being perfect for 3 weeks. Set a standard you can actually hit most days and stick to it.
