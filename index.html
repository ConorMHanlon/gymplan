PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD

If you are not adding weight over time, you are not building muscle. Everything else is secondary.

1
Find your working weight
Pick a weight where 8 reps is genuinely hard and rep 9 would fail. That is your starting weight for each exercise.
2
Warm-up set
10-12 easy reps at ~50% of your working weight. Rest 2 minutes. This primes the muscle without fatiguing it.
3
Set 1 and Set 2 — 8 reps to near failure
All sets are taken to near failure — you physically stop at 8 because rep 9 is not possible with good form. Rest 2-3 minutes between each set.
4
Final set — push to failure
AMRAP — As Many Reps As Possible. Go until you cannot do another clean rep. Log the number. This is your benchmark.
5
The progression rule
If you can complete 8 reps but feel you could physically manage a 9th — the weight is too light. Increase it.
The target weight is whatever makes 8 reps genuinely your limit. If a 9th rep is possible, add weight.

Repeat this forever. That is progressive overload.
6
Deload every 6 weeks
Drop weight by 20% for one full week. Joints and the nervous system need it. You will come back stronger.

TIPS & STRATEGY

The training is sorted. Here is everything else that will actually move the needle.

NUTRITION — THE BASICS

Find your calorie target
Multiply your weight in lbs by 14-18 depending on activity. Sedentary desk job = multiply by 14-15. That is your maintenance. Eat 20% below that number to lose fat while keeping muscle. Example: 180 lbs x 15 = 2,700 maintenance. 2,700 x 0.8 = 2,160 kcal target.
Protein — the only macro that truly matters
Aim for 0.7-1g per lb of bodyweight daily. At this intake you can build muscle while losing fat simultaneously — body recomposition. Without enough protein, any calorie deficit just eats muscle instead of fat. Chicken, eggs, Greek yoghurt, tuna, cottage cheese, protein shakes.
Change the ratio, not just the quantity
Make protein the main event on your plate and carbs the side dish — not the other way around. You do not need to eliminate carbs. Just flip the proportions. This one change alone has a significant impact without requiring calorie counting.
Avoid liquid calories
Soda, juice, energy drinks, and fancy coffees can add 500+ kcal without you noticing or feeling full. Switch to water, black coffee, or zero sugar alternatives. This is often the single easiest calorie cut available.
Have 2-3 default meals
Pick 2-3 high protein meals you enjoy, calculate their calories once, and rotate them. Decision fatigue kills diets. When you already know what you are eating, you remove the moment of weakness where you reach for something easy and bad.
Do not keep junk in the house
You do not need willpower if the food is not there. Willpower is a finite resource — remove it from the equation entirely. If it is not in the fridge, you cannot eat it at 11pm.

LIFESTYLE — THE MULTIPLIERS

Sleep — your secret weapon
You are getting 7-8 hrs — protect this at all costs. Muscle is built during sleep, not during the workout. Poor sleep raises cortisol, tanks testosterone, kills motivation, and makes you significantly more likely to binge eat the next day. It is not optional.
Walk every day
10,000 steps a day is one of the highest return things you can do. It burns calories without spiking hunger the way running does, lowers stress, and improves recovery between sessions. You do not need to go fast. A slow 30-40 minute walk counts.
Stress is making you fat
High cortisol from chronic stress drives fat storage, kills testosterone, and triggers binge eating. Managing stress — whether through walking, sleep, reducing commitments, or simply not skipping rest days — is a legitimate fat loss strategy.
The two-day rule
Never miss two sessions or two good eating days in a row. One bad day is totally normal and does not matter. Two in a row starts building a new bad habit in the opposite direction. Get back on it the next day, every time.

MINDSET — THE LONG GAME

Identity over goals
"I want to lose 10 kg" is a goal. Goals end. "I am someone who takes care of their health" is an identity. Identities do not end. Make decisions based on who you want to be, not what you want to weigh, and the results follow automatically — and stick.
You can still eat the good stuff
Pizza, ice cream, a night out — none of these derail you if your overall week is solid. Proving to yourself that this lifestyle is enjoyable is what keeps you consistent for months and years, not weeks. There are no forbidden foods, only context.
80% adherence beats 100% perfection
The person who is consistent 80% of the time for 6 months will always beat the person who is perfect for 3 weeks and then quits. Lower the bar enough that you can clear it every day. Progress compounds — even slow progress.
Sustainability is the strategy
The best diet is the one you can actually maintain. The best training plan is the one you actually show up to. If you hate what you are doing, you will not do it for long. Optimise for something you can keep doing indefinitely, not something that gets fast results.
